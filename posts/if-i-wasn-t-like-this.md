If I wasn’t like this,  
if I didn’t feel so much,  
want so much,  
need so much —   
maybe I’d finally be okay.

If I didn’t crave a girlfriend,  
didn’t feel this ache to be loved, seen, chosen —   
maybe I wouldn’t come off desperate.  
Maybe I’d be calm.  
Collected.  
Like the ones who don’t try so hard  
because they don’t care as much.  
Because they never needed love to breathe.

If I didn’t carry this anxiety —   
in my voice, my hands, my timing,  
my silences that come too early or too late —   
maybe I’d be better with people.  
Maybe I’d know what to say.  
Maybe I wouldn’t second-guess every moment  
and punish myself after.

If I had a good mindset —   
like they say everyone can have —   
I wouldn’t be here.  
Wallowing.  
Looping.  
Hating how I feel,  
but unable to feel anything else.

If I wasn’t me,  
maybe I’d be grateful.  
Maybe I’d be light.  
Maybe I’d know how to stay happy  
without breaking open every few weeks  
under the weight of my own thoughts.

If I wasn’t like this…

…but I am.

And I don’t know if I’m wrong.  
I just know it hurts.  
That I can’t just flip a switch and be better.  
That my need to love and be loved  
feels like both a flame and a curse.  
That I want peace,  
but my mind makes war out of every quiet moment.

If I wasn’t like this,  
I’d be someone else.  
Someone simpler.  
Maybe happier.  
But not me.

And for now —   
all I can do is try  
to make peace  
with this version of myself  
who feels too much  
and still hopes anyway.
