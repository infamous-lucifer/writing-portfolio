**What do I even feel?**

Middle of the day,  
a thought slides in —   
introspection will always be late to the scene.  
By the time I name the fire,  
I’ve already burned something down.

I try to catch myself  
in the act of being.  
Try to analyze while I’m  
feeling,  
but self-awareness is a trailing ghost —   
arrives with the smoke, not the spark.

So I move on instinct.  
Raw. Unnamed.  
Sharp turns in silence.  
Bodily choices that feel like  
gut wisdom…  
but might just be  
anxiety wearing a wolf’s pelt.

I sit near a girl I like —   
whole day.  
No words. Just this  
heat in my chest  
and this ache in my jaw —   
I want to kiss her.  
But I don’t move.  
Just flinch inward.  
Again.

Then someone else walks up to her —   
and I shrink.  
A blade under my ribs  
named jealousy.  
But it’s not clean like that.  
It’s jealousy braided with  
longing,  
performance,  
self-doubt,  
a silent wish to disappear,  
and a louder one to be seen.

She isn’t even wearing shoes.  
And I’m noticing her feet.  
And then I’m noticing myself —   
noticing her feet.  
And I don’t know what that means.  
That thought alone startles me.

Wanting to talk to her.  
Not talking to her.  
Carving to be wanted.  
Not knowing how to make  
the wanting beautiful.

**And then —**

the world shifts,  
from heart to hustle.

First one to arrive at the office.  
Empty chairs and a timer ticking.  
I set the timer like  
discipline might summon focus.  
But I wait…  
until someone else comes.  
Then I start.

Why?

No answer.

Work is a storm of second-guessing.  
Am I too slow?  
Too dumb?  
Too afraid?  
What if this task meant 4 hours,  
and I stretch it across 2 days?  
Will my manager see a mistake,  
or a person drowning?

My mind is a room  
where 16 televisions scream  
different fears —   
and I’m just holding the remote,  
clicking mute,  
but never off.

So many feelings  
with no names.  
No exit routes.  
Just flickers.  
Just instincts.  
Just something tight behind the eyes  
and heavy in the gut.

I want to write them down.  
Frame them.  
Carve structure out of chaos.  
Make this hurt mean something.

But sometimes,  
it’s just a blur.

A blur of:

Wanting to kiss.  
Not kissing.  
Wanting to work.  
Not working.  
Wanting to be great.  
But doubting the hands  
I’ve been given.

So I ask again —   
not for a label,  
not for an answer,  
but just to keep my hands in the clay:  
**what emotions do I have?**

And there’s me.  
Not quite in the moment.  
But not out of it either.

Trying to write it  
not to explain  
but to survive it.

And maybe that’s what this is.

Not a poem.

Not a confession.

Just  
me —   
reaching for something  
that won’t let itself be held.
