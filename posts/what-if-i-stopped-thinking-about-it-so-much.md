I think my real problem is thinking about it —   
too much.  
Love.  
Friendship.  
What I don’t have.  
What I fear I’ll never get.

But when I look at my life —   
really look at it —   
I’m doing okay.  
I’m moving. Working. Learning. Showing up.  
Maybe not shining in every area,  
but I’m not collapsing either.

So why does this one area —   
love, connection, companionship —   
get to define everything?

Maybe the thinking itself is the trap.  
The constant inner debates.  
The association with social anxiety,  
with labels, with pain and stories  
I keep retelling until they become cages.

What if I chose something else?

What if I stopped trying to fix what’s missing  
and started noticing what’s whole?

What if — even if it felt fake at first —   
I wrote about the things I like about myself?

What if I named the parts that make me proud,  
even if they sound like bragging?  
Even if the voice inside calls it “narcissistic”?

Because maybe it’s not.  
Maybe it’s repair.

Maybe I need a story where I’m not just  
the wounded boy  
but the man who’s trying.  
The man who’s more than pain.  
The man who is building something —   
even if he still breaks sometimes.

And maybe…  
maybe that version deserves a voice, too.
