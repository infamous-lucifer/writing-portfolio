We choose actions because of how they make us feel, who we want to be, and what aligns with our own values. Even when we care for someone else, that caring comes from a place we’ve decided matters to us.<p id="ceb8" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Recognizing this doesn’t mean ignoring others or becoming selfish, it means seeing that our choices are ours. It gives us agency: we’re not just reacting to other people’s needs or fears, we’re acting because its who we want to be. That’s what makes love and connection stronger not weaker, because they’re chosen, not done out of pressure or panic.<p id="bbef" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p graf--trailing"><strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong"><em class="markup--em markup--p-em">— Akshat</em></strong>
