Every person has their own story.

Different backgrounds.  
Different battles.  
Different strengths.  
Different timelines.

And yet, all of us — without exception — carry the desire  
to be the protagonist of our own story.

But are we?

Because let’s be honest —

There are people more impressive than you.  
More successful.  
More followed.  
More loved.  
More everything.

So can you still feel like the main character in a world  
where others are flying,  
and you — you’re still walking…  
maybe even stuck?

They say everyone has their own story.

But do you?

Because sometimes it feels like you're not the author —  
you're the blank face in someone else's chapter.  
Not even a side character. Just a placeholder.

It's not like you haven’t done anything.  
You are doing things. You’re trying.  
You will keep trying.

But when you're sprinting and others are soaring —  
when you’re trapped in constancy  
and they keep crossing milestones —  
something inside you quietly asks:

“Is this my story… or just a role I’ve been handed?”

These aren’t jealous thoughts.  
They’re not even resentful.  
They’re just honest.

Maybe they’ve done more than you.  
Maybe not.

But in the quiet —  
in the stillness between steps —  
you feel it:

You’re not there.  
Not yet.  
Not enough.  
Not… you.
