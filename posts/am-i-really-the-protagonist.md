</figure><p>Every person has their own story.</p><p>Different backgrounds.<br>Different battles.<br>Different strengths.<br>Different timelines.</p><p>And yet, all of us — without exception — carry the desire<br>to be the protagonist of our own story.</p><p>But are we?</p><p>Because let’s be honest —</p><p>There are people more impressive than you.<br>More successful.<br>More followed.<br>More loved.<br>More everything.</p><p>So can you still feel like the main character in a world<br>where others are flying,<br>and you — you’re still walking…<br>maybe even stuck?</p><p>They say everyone has their own story.</p><p>But do you?</p><p>Because sometimes it feels like you're not the author —<br>you're the blank face in someone else's chapter.<br>Not even a side character. Just a placeholder.</p><p>It's not like you haven’t done anything.<br>You are doing things. You’re trying.<br>You will keep trying.</p><p>But when you're sprinting and others are soaring —<br>when you’re trapped in constancy<br>and they keep crossing milestones —<br>something inside you quietly asks:</p><p>“Is this my story… or just a role I’ve been handed?”</p><p>These aren’t jealous thoughts.<br>They’re not even resentful.<br>They’re just honest.</p><p>Maybe they’ve done more than you.<br>Maybe not.</p><p>But in the quiet —<br>in the stillness between steps —<br>you feel it:</p><p>You’re not there.<br>Not yet.<br>Not enough.<br>Not… you.</p>
