Maybe I’m not the right person for her.  
If she can’t tell me what she feels —   
if her love hides behind glances and silences,  
if I have to decode her instead of simply be told —   
then maybe I’m not the one she feels safe enough to be real with.

And that’s okay.

Because she deserves someone  
who makes her forget she ever had to hide.  
Someone she can say _“I like you”_ or _“I love you”_ to  
without rehearsing it first in her head.  
Without fear.

I don’t want a love that tests me.  
I want a love that meets me.  
Fully. Freely. Without disguise.

And I want to be someone  
who makes another feel safe enough  
to bring out their best self —   
their softest thoughts, their tangled truths, their trembling honesty.

If I can’t be that space for her,  
then maybe we aren’t the right fit.

She deserves someone she can fall apart with  
and feel held, not judged.  
And I deserve a love that doesn’t make me guess —   
just opens its hands… and stays.
