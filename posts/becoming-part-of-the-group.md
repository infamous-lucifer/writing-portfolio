I used to think I was being real.  
That my silence was a form of depth.  
That not laughing at surface jokes meant I wasn’t entertaining shallowness.  
That choosing one-on-one vulnerability over group noise meant I was brave.

I was wrong.  
And I know it now.

Because something’s changed.

It’s strange —   
I feel conscious, yet slightly outside myself.  
Not like I’m dissociating.  
No. It’s fully _me_ — just not this me.  
Not the one typing, analyzing, thinking.  
But the other one —   
The version of me that exists when I’m _with them_.

He’s different.  
Not fake.  
Just a different _expression_ of me.

He reacts faster.  
Talks lighter.  
Smiles more.  
He belongs in a way this quiet, introspective self never did.

And the strange part?  
I don’t hate him.  
In fact…  
I think I might be proud of him.

Maybe vulnerability isn’t always about confessions and trauma.  
Maybe it’s about letting your guard down in a room full of noise,  
and not needing to be the deepest person there.

Maybe vulnerability is walking into the group —   
with your awkwardness, your hesitations, your silly jokes —   
and still _choosing to belong_.

No, I haven’t brought profound thoughts to the group.  
I haven’t made them pause and reflect.  
But I’ve brought something else.

A friendliness.  
A warmth.  
A kind of presence that, while maybe not _essential_,  
still changes the chemistry of the room just a little.

And maybe…  
that’s enough.
