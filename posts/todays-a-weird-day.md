sad — <br>but because I didn’t.</p><p name="7057" id="7057" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">No ache in my chest.<br>No urgency to be loved.<br>No inner monologue begging for connection or attention or someone to miss me.</p><p name="64ba" id="64ba" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Just… quiet.</p><p name="010c" id="010c" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">And it feels strange.<br>Unsettling, long, pain was the map.<br>The familiar place I returned to.<br>The ache was my identity.<br>The longing gave me direction.</p><p name="0eb2" id="0eb2" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Now without it — <br>Who am I?</p><p name="f7f5" id="f7f5" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Is this peace?<br>Or is this what numbness disguised as healing looks like?</p><p name="b3c9" id="b3c9" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">I don’t know.<br>But I know this much:</p><p name="d57a" id="d57a" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">There’s something terrifying about not knowing what to do with yourself<br>when you’re no longer trying to survive.</p><p name="02df" id="02df" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Because I used to understand pain.<br>I knew its rhythm.<br>I knew how to write about it, cry through it, sit with it until it softened.</p><p name="1c32" id="1c32" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">But this?<br>This stillness?<br>This emptiness?</p><p name="855d" id="855d" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">It’s like walking through a room with no furniture, no memories, no sound — <br>just blank space.</p><p name="5441" id="5441" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">And a part of me — <br>a stubborn, aching part — <br>wants to run back into sadness.<br>Because at least there,<br>I knew what I was going through.</p><p name="4114" id="4114" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Today is weird.<br>Because I realized I had made pain my home.</p><p name="32d7" id="32d7" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p graf--trailing">And now that it’s gone…<br>I don’t know where I live anymore.</p>
