id="69e8" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">Today’s a weird day.<br>Not because I woke up sad — <br>but because I didn’t.<p id="7057" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">No ache in my chest.<br>No urgency to be loved.<br>No inner monologue begging for connection or attention or someone to miss me.<p id="64ba" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Just… quiet.<p id="010c" class="graf graf-after--p">Now without it — <br>Who am I?<p id="f7f5" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Is this peace?<br>Or is this what numbness disguised as healing looks like?<p id="b3c9" class="graf graf--p trying to survive.<p id="02df" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Because I used to understand pain.<br>I knew its rhythm.<br>I knew how to write about it, cry through it, sit with it until it softened.<p id="1c32" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">But this?<br>This stillness?<br>This emptiness?<p id="855d" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">It’s like walking through a room with no furniture, no memories, no sound — <br>just blank space.<p id="5441" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">And a part of me — <br>a stubborn, aching part — <br>wants to run back into sadness.<br>Because at least there,<br>I knew what I was going through.<p id="4114" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Today is weird.<br>Because I realized I had made pain my home.<p id="32d7" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p graf--trailing">And now that it’s gone…<br>I don’t know where I live anymore.
