</figure><p>Today’s a weird day.<br>Not because I woke up sad — <br>but because I didn’t.</p><p>No ache in my chest.<br>No urgency to be loved.<br>No inner monologue begging for connection or attention or someone to miss me.</p><p>Just… quiet.</p><p>And it feels strange.<br>Unsettling, even.</p><p>Not because I’m hurting — <br>but because I’m not.</p><p>Because for so long, pain was the map.<br>The familiar place I returned to.<br>The ache was my identity.<br>The longing gave me direction.</p><p>Now without it — <br>Who am I?</p><p>Is this peace?<br>Or is this what numbness disguised as healing looks like?</p><p>I don’t know.<br>But I know this much:</p><p>There’s something terrifying about not knowing what to do with yourself<br>when you’re no longer trying to survive.</p><p>Because I used to understand pain.<br>I knew its rhythm.<br>I knew how to write about it, cry through it, sit with it until it softened.</p><p>But this?<br>This stillness?<br>This emptiness?</p><p>It’s like walking through a room with no furniture, no memories, no sound — <br>just blank space.</p><p>And a part of me — <br>a stubborn, aching part — <br>wants to run back into sadness.<br>Because at least there,<br>I knew what I was going through.</p><p>Today is weird.<br>Because I realized I had made pain my home.</p><p>And now that it’s gone…<br>I don’t know where I live anymore.</p>
