### **What I find hardest about starting things**

It’s not the start itself that’s hard.

It’s the part before — where I convince myself not to even want it.

It starts with money.

“Can I afford this?”

“Will this add to my already heavy load?”

“Is this even worth it?”

Then come the deeper doubts:

Even if money weren’t an issue, I’d still wonder — am I even good enough for this?

What if I’m just not built for it?

The problem is, those thoughts don’t protect joy — they just delay it.

If doubt ever led to peace, maybe it’d be worth it. But it doesn’t.

They leave me miserable.

Because if I don’t let myself want, what’s the point of all this effort?

What’s the point of surviving life if I never get to feel nature, take risks, or follow desire just for its own sake?

What’s hardest isn’t time or money — it’s permission.

Most problems can be solved. But you can’t solve what you won’t even let yourself want.

### What helps me finally act

#### 1\. Impulse

Honestly, sometimes it’s just impulse.

The desire builds quietly in the background, until suddenly — I act.

Even when I told myself I wouldn’t.

#### 2\. Planning

Other times, I wait.

If I really want it, I’ll slow down, think it through, and plan.

I won’t rush — but I won’t abandon it either.

Whether it’s impulse or a slow plan, it never starts until I allow myself to want.

And maybe that’s the real beginning — permission, not action.
