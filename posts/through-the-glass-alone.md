rel="photo-source noopener" target="_blank">Unsplash</a></figcaption></figure><p name="242b" id="242b" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">A lone being in a land of familiar strangers.<br>Each smile is known.<br>Each voice memorized.<br>And yet — <br>none of them carry me in their inner world.</p><p name="a7c7" id="a7c7" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">They pass through mine like echoes.<br>I remain untouched.<br>Not invisible.<br>Just unread.</p><p name="d578" id="d578" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">And I wonder — <br>am I meant to be known?<br>Or just translated endlessly,<br>never spoken back to?</p><p name="be43" id="be43" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">It’s like I live inside my head.<br>And outside it, there’s glass.<br>And beyond that — people.</p><p name="2be9" id="2be9" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">I can understand what they’re feeling.<br>I can read their faces, predict their silences.<br>But they still feel like thoughts in my mind — <br>not realities I can hold.</p><p name="ec27" id="ec27" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Like lanterns glowing far off in the dark.<br>I can see the light,<br>but never feel the warmth.</p><p name="a634" id="a634" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">And I ask myself:<br>But isn’t it true in itself?<br>Others are the thoughts of my world.<br>I know they have their own world — <br>but our realities never intersect.</p><p name="9518" id="9518" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">I know they are human.<br>I know they have identities, emotions, entire lives.<br>But I can’t process that truth beyond the surface.<br>I don’t land in their world,<br>and they don’t land in mine.</p><p name="d288" id="d288" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">They exist for me — <br>inside my own perception.<br>Fragments. Reflections. Not full presence.</p><p name="09b0" id="09b0" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">And emotionally — <br>it feels like madness.<br>Because you can sit beside someone,<br>touch them, joke with them,<br>spend hours or years — <br>and still not know<br>if they’ve ever really held you in their thoughts<br>the way you hold them in yours.</p><p name="9b53" id="9b53" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">That kind of detachment isn’t cold — <br>it’s quiet. Unbearably quiet.<br>Like you are the only real thing<br>in a room full of shadows that mimic life.</p><p name="bfb3" id="bfb3" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">And then I wonder — <br>can I ever fall in love?</p><p name="7cca" id="7cca" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Not because I lack care or curiosity.<br>But because I cannot yet feel someone<br>the way I feel myself.</p><p name="e1ca" id="e1ca" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">They could share everything with me — <br>their past, their fears, their softest stories — <br>and still,<br>I might not love them.</p><p name="533a" id="533a" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Not because they aren’t worthy,<br>but because I don’t yet have the space<br>in my cognition<br>for someone else to truly live.</p><p name="f318" id="f318" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Right now,<br>the only world I know how to fully inhabit<br>is my own.</p><p name="ff54" id="ff54" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p graf--trailing">And maybe…<br>maybe that’s what loneliness really is.<br>Not the absence of people — <br>but the inability<br>to let them exist<br>beyond the glass.</p>
