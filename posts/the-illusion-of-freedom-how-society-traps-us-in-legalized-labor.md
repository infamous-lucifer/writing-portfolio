We’re told we have choices. “Follow your passion,” they say, as if opportunities are just lying around, waiting for us to grab them. But are they? Or is this an illusion — a clever setup to keep us grinding, to make us willingly trade our dreams for mere survival?

Let’s be honest: even passion isn’t free. You can’t just love what you do; you have to monetize it. Your joy has to sell. Your creativity has to profit. Somewhere in that process, something pure, something essential, is lost. It’s not just about doing what you love — it’s about whether it pays. That’s the tragedy. Our dreams have become commodities.

**The System’s Design: A Safety Net or a Trap?**  
This system wasn’t built to help you dream; it was built to keep you in line. It’s for people who don’t have the privilege or resources to think beyond survival. What it offers isn’t opportunity — it’s compliance disguised as security.

Think about it. This structure that promises stability? It turns you into a cog in a machine. You’re not a person — you’re a job title, a LinkedIn profile, a performance review. The system doesn’t want you to live; it wants you to grind.

And for what?

**The Tragic Irony**  
As kids, we didn’t dream of filling out spreadsheets or chasing KPIs. We wanted to create, explore, and feel alive. But the world convinces us that those dreams are impractical. It teaches us to equate hard work with happiness, to believe we’ll find joy only after we’ve earned it.

But by the time we “earn” it, the best years of our lives are gone. And the system wins again.

**A System for the “Average” Person**  
Some argue that this system is necessary. They say it’s for people who can’t carve their own paths. Maybe they’re right — but even so, it doesn’t justify how the system traps everyone, no matter their potential.

Even if you follow your passion, the system forces you to fit it into its framework. Your creativity becomes currency. Your joy becomes a product. And those who can’t conform? They get survival, nothing more. No freedom, no joy — just existence.

**Breaking the Cycle**  
This isn’t how it’s supposed to be. Life isn’t meant to be an endless grind. It’s meant to be lived. To break free, we have to question the system:

*   **Redefine Success**: Forget society’s version of success. Focus on what truly fulfills you.
*   **Minimize Wants**: The less you need, the freer you become. Simplicity is liberation.
*   **Protect Passion**: If you must monetize your passion, do it your way. Don’t let the system kill your joy.
*   **Challenge the Norms**: Start asking questions. Why do we live this way? Who does it really benefit?

**The Bigger Picture**  
Sometimes I stop and think: is this what life is supposed to be? This endless cycle of work, survival, and waiting for “someday” to truly live? It feels absurd.

The system has stolen our ability to dream freely, to live authentically. But I refuse to give up. I want to believe we can rewrite the narrative. That we can create lives filled with meaning and joy, despite the system’s grip.

Life isn’t just about survival. It’s about living. And if the system won’t allow that, maybe it’s time we stop playing by its rules.
