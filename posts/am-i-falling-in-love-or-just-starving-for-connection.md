<h3 name="2df9" id="2df9" class="graf graf--h3 graf--leading graf--title">Am I Falling in Love, or Just Starving for Connection?</h3><h4 name="b05e" id="b05e" class="graf graf--h4 graf-after--h3 graf--subtitle">A confession about how I keep mistaking emotional hunger for intimacy — and the illusions I build in the name of&nbsp;love.</h4><figure name="100b" id="100b" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--h4"><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="0*ekN88IAj7ZSZKGio" data-width="2865" data-height="1910" data-unsplash-photo-id="Pv5WeEyxMWU" data-is-featured="true" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/800/0*ekN88IAj7ZSZKGio"><figcaption class="imageCaption">Photo by <a href="https://unsplash.com/@sashafreemind?utm_source=medium&amp;utm_medium=referral" data-href="https://unsplash.com/@sashafreemind?utm_source=medium&amp;utm_medium=referral" class="markup--anchor markup--figure-anchor" rel="photo-creator noopener" target="_blank">Sasha Freemind</a> on&nbsp;<a href="https://unsplash.com?utm_source=medium&amp;utm_medium=referral" data-href="https://unsplash.com?utm_source=medium&amp;utm_medium=referral" class="markup--anchor markup--figure-anchor" rel="photo-source noopener" target="_blank">Unsplash</a></figcaption></figure><p name="1212" id="1212" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">Sometimes I wonder if I’ve ever actually fallen in love.<br>Not because I haven’t felt things — I’ve felt them intensely. So intensely, it scared me.<br>But when I look closely, it wasn’t the person that moved me.<br>It was what they made me feel — or rather, what I hoped they might help me feel.</p><p name="0588" id="0588" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">The feeling of being seen.<br>The possibility of being chosen.<br>The illusion of safety in someone else’s gaze.</p><p name="b557" id="b557" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">That’s what I chase.</p><p name="75d9" id="75d9" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">It’s not wrong to want connection.<br>It’s deeply human — necessary, even.<br>But I think I confuse connection with something else.<br>With rescue. With repair. With relief.</p><p name="304b" id="304b" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">If someone shows me even a flicker of kindness, I build an entire emotional ecosystem around it.<br>I fall in love with the version of them I create — the one who finally sees me.<br>Not the actual person.<br>I don’t even know the actual person.</p><p name="ec5e" id="ec5e" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">It feels constructed. Not organic.<br>Even as a kid, I remember choosing who to crush on.<br>It didn’t strike me out of nowhere. I decided.<br>Because I wanted to be someone who felt love.<br>Because feeling love made me feel real.</p><p name="5ae1" id="5ae1" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">And so I keep falling — not for people, but for the idea of mattering to someone.<br>It’s not love. It’s emotional hunger dressed up in romantic hope.</p><p name="3415" id="3415" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">It’s everywhere in me.<br>Even when I’m writing messages to colleagues or friends, I assign them roles:<br>The office mom. The motivator. The big sister.<br>I don’t just appreciate them. I turn them into emotional symbols.</p><p name="9ab8" id="9ab8" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">A role they never agreed to.<br>A placeholder in my inner story.</p><p name="7ce5" id="7ce5" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">It makes things neat, manageable, emotionally structured.<br>But it also traps me. Because the moment someone steps outside their role — or never fills it the way I hoped — I feel betrayed.<br>Not by them.<br>But by my own fantasy.</p><p name="c266" id="c266" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">That’s the core wound:<br>My craving to be known, combined with my fear of being rejected once I’m fully visible.<br>So I create people in my head instead.<br>Versions of them that can’t leave.<br>Versions of love I can control.</p><p name="2628" id="2628" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">But the price of that is real connection.<br>And the weight of that illusion is starting to suffocate me.</p><p name="ebb2" id="ebb2" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">I want to stop doing this.<br>Not stop needing love — <br>but stop making strangers carry the burden of my unmet needs.</p><p name="2bc5" id="2bc5" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">I want to stop mistaking hunger for intimacy.<br>Stop turning warmth into promises it never made.<br>Stop trying to get from someone else what I’ve never learned to give myself.</p><p name="717f" id="717f" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">I want to learn to see people as they are.<br>Not as what they could mean to me.<br>Not as characters in a script I’m unconsciously writing.</p><p name="2fe2" id="2fe2" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p graf--trailing">I want to learn how to love without projection.<br>To let someone remain a mystery.<br>And maybe, to become one to myself again too.</p>
