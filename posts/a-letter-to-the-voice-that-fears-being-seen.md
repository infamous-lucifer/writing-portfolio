Hey.

I know you’re trying to help me.  
You’ve always tried.

You speak when I’m about to hit “publish,”  
when someone I know might see my post,  
when I write something too raw,  
too soft,  
too _me_.

You whisper things like:

> _“They’ll laugh at this.”  
> “This is too much.”  
> “You’re just being needy.”  
> “You’re giving them ammunition.”_

You don’t say these things because you’re cruel.  
You say them because you’re scared.

You remember the classrooms,  
the teasing,  
the silence after vulnerability,  
the way softness got met with smirks or shrugs or nothing at all.

And you vowed: _Never again._  
Never let them see that part of us.  
Never let them get close enough to hurt it.

And for a while, you were right.  
You kept me hidden.  
Safe.  
Small.  
Unchanged.

But now?

I’m not a child trying to survive schoolyards.  
I’m not a teenager trying to decode stares.  
I’m someone who writes — who **needs** to write — because it’s the only way to stay real.

And yes, I still feel afraid.  
Afraid that they’ll judge me.  
Afraid that I’m too much and not enough at the same time.

But here’s what you don’t see:

I’ve survived their silence.  
I’ve survived their misunderstanding.  
And still, I have something worth saying.

So to you, my scared voice —   
You can stay.  
You can whisper.  
You can tremble in the corner when I speak out.

But you don’t get to hold the pen anymore.

I write now.

And I write not because it’s safe,  
but because it’s _true_.

Because if I don’t,  
I’ll lose myself in the hiding you call protection.

So thank you for keeping me alive.  
But I’m here to do more than survive now.

I’m here to _be seen._

_— Akshat_
