</figure><p>Hey.</p><p>I know you’re trying to help me.<br>You’ve always tried.</p><p>You speak when I’m about to hit “publish,”<br>when someone I know might see my post,<br>when I write something too raw,<br>too soft,<br>too <em>me</em>.</p><p>You whisper things like:</p><blockquote><em>“They’ll laugh at this.”<br>“This is too much.”<br>“You’re just being needy.”<br>“You’re giving them ammunition.”</em></blockquote><p>You don’t say these things because you’re cruel.<br>You say them because you’re scared.</p><p>You remember the classrooms,<br>the teasing,<br>the silence after vulnerability,<br>the way softness got met with smirks or shrugs or nothing at all.</p><p>And you vowed: <em>Never again.</em><br>Never let them see that part of us.<br>Never let them get close enough to hurt it.</p><p>And for a while, you were right.<br>You kept me hidden.<br>Safe.<br>Small.<br>Unchanged.</p><p>But now?</p><p>I’m not a child trying to survive schoolyards.<br>I’m not a teenager trying to decode stares.<br>I’m someone who writes — who <strong>needs</strong> to write — because it’s the only way to stay real.</p><p>And yes, I still feel afraid.<br>Afraid that they’ll judge me.<br>Afraid that I’m too much and not enough at the same time.</p><p>But here’s what you don’t see:</p><p>I’ve survived their silence.<br>I’ve survived their misunderstanding.<br>And still, I have something worth saying.</p><p>So to you, my scared voice — <br>You can stay.<br>You can whisper.<br>You can tremble in the corner when I speak out.</p><p>But you don’t get to hold the pen anymore.</p><p>I write now.</p><p>And I write not because it’s safe,<br>but because it’s <em>true</em>.</p><p>Because if I don’t,<br>I’ll lose myself in the hiding you call protection.</p><p>So thank you for keeping me alive.<br>But I’m here to do more than survive now.</p><p>I’m here to <em>be seen.</em></p><p><em>— Akshat</em></p>
