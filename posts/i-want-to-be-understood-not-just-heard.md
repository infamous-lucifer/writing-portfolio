Most of us don’t even understand ourselves.

We walk through life with a sense of identity, sure — but much of what drives us operates below the surface: preferences shaped by childhood, biases inherited from culture, wounds dressed up as logic. You can say you “know yourself,” but throw yourself into a new situation — pain, love, betrayal, performance — and suddenly a new self emerges. One you didn’t expect.

So when I say I want to be understood by others, I know it’s a tall order.  
Maybe even an impossible one.  
Maybe… it shouldn’t even be expected.

But there’s something deeper underneath that longing — not for perfect understanding, but for **acceptance**.  
For someone to include you in their world without trying to correct your experience.  
To not be invalidated.  
To not be fixed.  
To simply be _held_ in your truth, even if that truth is still messy and half-formed.

In my case, I’d even say it’s a form of emotional exposureism.  
A desire not just to be heard, but to be **peeled open**.  
Not forcefully. Not clinically.  
But gently, curiously — by someone who actually wants to know what’s beneath the mask.

### The Listening That Heals

There’s a kind of listening that goes beyond words.  
A kind that feels like being understood, not just heard.

It’s not about advice. Not about responses.  
It’s about presence.  
You can feel it when someone is _with_ you in your emotional space, not just waiting for their turn to speak.

Ironically, most conversations aren’t like this.  
They fall into two categories: the meaningless, and the meaningful.

The meaningless talks fill the air but not the soul.  
They’re about gossip, surface updates, passive fun.  
They aren’t bad. But they don’t touch anything real.

Then there are the other ones.  
The talks that aren’t necessarily _productive_, but feel like oxygen.  
They’re not about the outer world — they’re about _you_.  
Your internal weather. Your fears. Your contradictions.  
They don’t always make sense.  
They don’t need to.

They **nourish the spirit**, not the to-do list.  
And in those moments, you feel understood — not because someone solved you, but because they stayed long enough to witness you unfolding.

### The Contradiction I Carry

Maybe that’s the contradiction behind the title.  
I say I want to be understood — but I also know I’m constantly changing.  
I say I want someone to get me — but I also doubt anyone can.

Still, the desire persists.  
Not to be decoded like a puzzle, but to be embraced like a poem.

So maybe I don’t want full understanding.  
Maybe what I want is **a willingness to try**.  
To sit with my emotional complexity and not flinch.

To say:

> “I don’t fully get it, but I’m here. Keep going.”

That alone can be the most healing sentence in the world.

### Final Thought

> Being understood isn’t about someone seeing all of you — it’s about them wanting to.
