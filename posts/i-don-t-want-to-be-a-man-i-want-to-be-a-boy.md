**World:**  
“He’s just a boy. Not a man.”  
You can hear it in their voice — disappointment dressed as concern.  
As if “boy” is a disease I haven’t outgrown.

**Me:**  
Maybe I am.  
Maybe I want to stay that way.

**World:**  
“A man doesn’t cry like that.”  
“A man doesn’t need reassurance.”  
“A man doesn’t play. He builds.”

**Me:**  
But I miss playing.  
I miss doing things not because they’re useful,  
but because they make my heart skip a beat.  
I want to make mistakes and not have it define my worth.  
I want to love _just because_ — not because it’s safe or earned or deserved.

I want to do silly things.  
Like putting up cat wallpapers.  
Like laughing too loud.  
Like lying on the floor without explaining why.

**World:**  
“That’s immature.”  
“You’ll never be respected like this.”  
“You can’t need people this much.”

**Me:**  
But I do.  
I need. I depend. I ache.  
Sometimes I get overwhelmed and don’t want to talk.  
Sometimes I want someone to choose me without me having to perform.

I know I’m not what you think a man should be.  
Maybe I’ll never be.

**World:**  
“Then you’ll be left behind.”  
“No one wants a grown-up child.”

**Me:**  
I know.  
And I feel it.  
When they don’t choose me.  
When I stay quiet in a group.  
When I sit in a corner just so I don’t have to fake toughness.

I feel the cost.  
But I also feel the truth.  
And the truth is:

I like who I am when I’m not trying to “be a man.”  
I like being a boy.

**A boy who’s honest.  
A boy who tries.  
A boy who feels before he filters.  
A boy who isn’t afraid to say: “I don’t know.”**

Let them call it weakness.  
Let them mistake it for failure.  
But one day —   
I hope someone meets this boy and says,

> “That’s exactly the kind of man I trust.”
