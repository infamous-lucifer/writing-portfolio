It all started with a talking pig. Not just any talking pig — my pet, Porky, who somehow knew all my secrets and had an unfortunate habit of revealing them to strangers.

I should have suspected something was off. Porky had always been… different. He would stare at people like he understood them, sigh dramatically when I made bad decisions, and somehow always found my hidden snacks. But I chalked it up to him being an unusually smart pig.

Then, one day, as we strolled through the city, Porky casually blurted out my childhood crush’s name to a passing businessman.

The man froze, adjusted his earpiece, and whispered something into his collar.

Turns out, he wasn’t a businessman. He was a secret agent. And he thought Porky was an advanced AI experiment that had escaped.

**“Sir, we need to confiscate your asset,”** the agent said, reaching for his belt.

I glanced at Porky. “Talk to me, buddy.”

To my horror, Porky suddenly started speaking in a language I didn’t recognize — something between ancient Latin and garbled pig squeals.

I blinked. “Ah, I see… It’s not AI. It’s an alien.”

The agent hesitated. “An alien?”

I leaned in, lowering my voice. “And I’ll tell you everything — but only if you make me an agent too.”

A long pause. Then he handed me a black card.

**“Welcome to the agency.”**

### Mission: Talking Cows

My first assignment? Investigate a secret underground society of talking cows. Naturally, I sent Porky in as a spy.

That was my first mistake.

Instead of infiltrating, Porky switched sides. He joined the cows and started a full-blown rebellion against humanity.

I tracked him down and tried to reason with him. “Porky, think this through. If you become king, you’ll have to handle diplomacy, paperwork, cow-related taxes… way too much effort.”

Porky blinked. “You’re right. Ruling sucks.”

But with Porky stepping down, the cows saw me as their leader. And with no better plan, I led them to graze and have fun.

That was my second mistake.

![](/assets/images/posts/1*XxO6WB7Zq4atSUthEB95Eg.jpeg.jpg)

### The Great Moo Movement

The world misunderstood. They thought I was leading a revolution. Governments panicked. Negotiators arrived, asking for my demands.

I gave them one: **“Freedom, fun, and no exploitation.”**

Shockingly, they agreed. But on one condition — I had to become the official ambassador of cow-human relations. My first duty? Attend a global summit. In a tuxedo.

Instead, I sent a cow in a tuxedo.

That was my third mistake.

Mid-speech, the cow panicked and started mooing uncontrollably. To salvage the situation, I did the only logical thing: I led the entire summit in synchronized mooing.

The media called it **The Great Moo Movement.**

From there, things escalated. People embraced the “cow lifestyle” — grazing, relaxing, and enjoying life. Productivity collapsed. The economy suffered. To fix it, I tied creativity to the movement. Art, music, and inventions flourished.

But then, extremists formed the **Moo Cult.**

To break their devotion, I revealed a fatal flaw in cows: **they panic.** Some cultists abandoned their faith. Others claimed panic was a divine test. To distract them, I appointed my tuxedo-wearing cow as their spiritual leader.

It worked. But then… the pigs wanted recognition.

![](/assets/images/posts/1*KxEZctXn2NwWXh1L3rFvjw.jpeg.jpg)

### The Animal Kingdom Rises

The pigs demanded a national holiday. I gave them one: **The Moo Movement Day.** But then the chickens got jealous. They plotted against me.

I responded… by eating them.

That was my fourth mistake.

The surviving chickens formed **The Pecking Order,** a rebel resistance. To defeat them, I took their eggs and convinced the new chicks their revolution had abandoned them. The chicks pledged loyalty to me. Furious, the rebel chickens built an entire chicken civilization to fight back.

At this point, I did what any reasonable leader would do: I signed a treaty.

Thus, **The Animal Kingdom** was born, with pigs, cows, and chickens ruling alongside me. But then humans felt left out.

So I made a declaration:

**“Humans are animals too.”**

Most people accepted it. But a faction of rebels, **The Apex Order,** refused to bow. To control them, I launched a fake resistance group under their name — allowing me to manipulate their every move.

With that, the world was mine.

And to think… it all started with a talking pig.
