**Ever wondered — in this world obsessed with importance —   
you’re just a normie?**

99.99% of people don’t matter.  
Cop, drug dealer, mayor of a forgotten city —   
they vanish.

Not to society.  
Not to history.  
Not to the universe.

And yet —

That 0.01% historical footprint?  
It isn’t a measure of meaning.  
It’s a measure of scale.

If you got hit by a car tomorrow,  
your parents’ world would collapse.

That’s not trivial.  
That’s not pages in a history book.  
That’s the death of a world.

**Meaning doesn’t have to be eternal to be real.**

It doesn’t matter if you’re ultra-rich or a beggar —   
stretch time far enough,  
and your name erodes the same way.

You, me, most of us —   
we’re inconsequential.  
Background noise.  
Forgettable.

Life inherently has no meaning.  
It doesn’t matter to anybody whether I’m a waste or a genius.  
Because everyone matters only to themselves.  
Just like the universe.

But that’s like judging a poem by the fact it ends.  
Or a kiss by the fact you’ll die.

**Brevity doesn’t erase value.**

So when they say,  
_“If life has no meaning, you give it one”_ —   
I ask:

**How?**

How do you give meaning  
when your heart is filled with bleakness?

How do you build significance  
when nothing inside feels worth offering?

When bleakness becomes your internal weather,  
meaning isn’t something you give —   
it’s something you sculpt,  
**slowly, painfully, in silence.**

You act in spite of the void.

Meaning is not a feeling.  
It’s not happiness.  
It’s **commitment.**

You choose to love when it’s easier to detach.  
You choose to create when it’s easier to rot.  
You choose to persist  
when silence says nothing will change.

**Meaning is born through resistance to meaninglessness.**

But still —   
how do you build a life  
when happiness is just comparison,  
and sadness is just shame?

You’ve seen the hollow structure of modern emotion:  
Happiness = _“I’m doing better than them.”_  
Sadness = _“I’m falling short.”_

**Yet there’s a way out —**   
Seek metrics beyond comparison.  
Ask:

*   Was I true to myself today?
*   Did I grow, even a little?
*   Did I face the void instead of numbing it?

### So What Now?

You already know life has no inherent meaning.  
But you’re not searching for meaning.

**You’re searching for a reason to believe  
your presence isn’t a mistake.**

You want to matter  
to someone.  
Something.  
Somewhere.

And that desire —   
quiet, honest, aching —   
**might just be the first real thing  
you’ve ever felt.**
