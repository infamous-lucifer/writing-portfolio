### The Starting Line Isn’t the Same

**Me:** “You ever wonder why we compare ourselves to people who were born into privilege?”

**You:** “Hmm… yeah, sometimes. Like when you see someone who’s already miles ahead because of where they started.”

**Me:** “Exactly. It’s like we forget that they didn’t start where we did. But here’s the thing — why do we expect our journeys to look the same when the starting points were never equal?”

**You:** “That’s true. It’s like comparing a sprinter who starts at the finish line with someone running a marathon from the back.”

**Me:** “And the worst part? Social media makes it worse. You’re scrolling through Instagram, and boom — there’s that success story that makes you question if you’re doing enough. But no one talks about the invisible head start that person had.”

**You:** “Yeah! And then you feel like you’re not working hard enough, but the truth is…”

**Me:** “The truth is, you’re probably working twice as hard — just without the safety net they had.”

### But Isn’t Hard Work Enough?

**You:** “But wait… isn’t hard work supposed to even things out?”

**Me:** “That’s the myth, isn’t it? We’ve been sold this idea that hard work alone will get you there. But in reality, hard work amplifies what you already have. If you have resources, connections, and security, hard work takes you even further. But if you’re building from scratch, you’re not just working hard — you’re fighting gravity.”

**You:** “So, success isn’t just about effort?”

**Me:** “Nope. It’s about leverage. And privilege gives you that leverage. Without it, every step is an uphill battle.”

### So, Why Do We Still Compare?

**You:** “But if this is so obvious… why do we still compare?”

**Me:** “Because we’re wired to. Society feeds us this idea of meritocracy — the belief that anyone can make it if they just work hard enough. But that’s not the full story. It ignores context.”

**You:** “Context?”

**Me:** “Yeah. Think about it. The rich kid who starts a successful business and the first-generation hustler who works their way up — they’re celebrated the same way. But one had the blueprint handed to them. The other? They had to draw the map while walking the path.”

### Does That Make Privileged Success Less Valid?

**You:** “So… does that mean success from privilege is less valid?”

**Me:** “Not at all. Success is success. But the weight of that success is different. Imagine lifting 100 pounds when you’re standing on a platform versus lifting it while climbing a mountain. The effort is different, and so is the journey.”

**You:** “And yet, both get applauded the same.”

**Me:** “Exactly. And that’s the injustice. It’s not about diminishing their success — it’s about acknowledging that not everyone starts with the same weight on their shoulders.”

### But Doesn’t It Mean More When You Build from Scratch?

**You:** “I’ve always felt that success feels different when you build it yourself.”

**Me:** “It does. And that’s the beauty of it. When you build from scratch, you’re not just succeeding — you’re changing the course of generations. That’s something privilege can’t replicate.”

**You:** “And that’s why I respect those stories more.”

**Me:** “Exactly. Because they didn’t just work hard — they had to create something where nothing existed. They built the foundation while others were handed one.”

### So, What’s the Real Definition of Success?

**You:** “Then what is success if it’s not just about money or status?”

**Me:** “Success is about taking control of your life despite the odds. It’s about creating a foundation where there wasn’t one before. It’s about building something that future generations will stand on.”

**You:** “So, it’s about legacy?”

**Me:** “Exactly. And the beauty of building from scratch is that your success doesn’t just benefit you — it rewrites the script for those who come after you.”

### Why This Conversation Needs to Be Shared

**You:** “I feel like more people need to hear this.”

**Me:** “Absolutely. Too many people are comparing their grind to someone born into privilege and feeling like they’re not enough. We need to remind them — their journey is valid. Their success carries the weight of generations. And that deserves more respect than any inherited wealth ever will.”

**You:** “Let’s share this with the world.”

**Me:** “Let’s do it. Because people need to know that true success isn’t measured by where you are — it’s measured by how far you’ve come.”

### And for Those Who Are Building from Scratch?

**You:** “And for those who are building from scratch?”

**Me:** “They’re already winning. They just don’t realize it yet.”
