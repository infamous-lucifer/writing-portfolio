I’m scared.

I want to send a message to the people in my life —   
my colleagues, my friends, my parents.  
I want to tell them what I see in them.  
How they’ve impacted me.  
How their presence, in small and quiet ways,  
has shaped the way I see the world and myself.

But I’m scared.

I’m scared they’ll think it’s fake.  
That it’ll sound awkward.  
That I’ll seem over-emotional.  
That some won’t feel the same way,  
or worse — won’t believe that it’s coming from my heart.

I imagine them reading it and cringing.  
I imagine them thinking, “What is this?”  
I imagine myself shrinking after I press send.

And yet…

It feels like something I have to say.

Not to get something from them.  
Not to be praised or validated.  
But because it’s true.  
Because I’ve carried these thoughts inside me for too long,  
and maybe they deserve to live outside me for once.

You know that feeling when a kid writes something for class —   
a poem, a letter, a little paragraph —   
and they’re nervous about showing it to their parents?  
Not because it’s wrong,  
but because it’s real.

That’s how I feel.

Like I’ve scribbled something honest in a notebook,  
and now I’m walking around with it in my hand,  
unsure if I should pass it to the people it’s meant for.

And I wonder…  
don’t others feel this way, too?

Don’t others hesitate before saying something kind?  
Something vulnerable?  
Something that cracks the surface we all wear?

It’s so easy to keep these things hidden.  
To lock them away in the name of pride or fear or timing.

But I don’t want to hide anymore.  
Even if my voice trembles.  
Even if my timing’s off.  
Even if they don’t say anything back.

Because saying it —   
speaking from that raw, awkward, trembling place —   
matters to me.

Even if no one else sees it.  
Even if it lands weird.  
Even if I blush with embarrassment after.

Maybe that’s okay.

Maybe that’s human.

And maybe…  
this time, I’ll say it anyway.
