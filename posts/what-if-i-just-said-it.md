</figure><p>I’m scared.</p><p>I want to send a message to the people in my life — <br>my colleagues, my friends, my parents.<br>I want to tell them what I see in them.<br>How they’ve impacted me.<br>How their presence, in small and quiet ways,<br>has shaped the way I see the world and myself.</p><p>But I’m scared.</p><p>I’m scared they’ll think it’s fake.<br>That it’ll sound awkward.<br>That I’ll seem over-emotional.<br>That some won’t feel the same way,<br>or worse — won’t believe that it’s coming from my heart.</p><p>I imagine them reading it and cringing.<br>I imagine them thinking, “What is this?”<br>I imagine myself shrinking after I press send.</p><p>And yet…</p><p>It feels like something I have to say.</p><p>Not to get something from them.<br>Not to be praised or validated.<br>But because it’s true.<br>Because I’ve carried these thoughts inside me for too long,<br>and maybe they deserve to live outside me for once.</p><p>You know that feeling when a kid writes something for class — <br>a poem, a letter, a little paragraph — <br>and they’re nervous about showing it to their parents?<br>Not because it’s wrong,<br>but because it’s real.</p><p>That’s how I feel.</p><p>Like I’ve scribbled something honest in a notebook,<br>and now I’m walking around with it in my hand,<br>unsure if I should pass it to the people it’s meant for.</p><p>And I wonder…<br>don’t others feel this way, too?</p><p>Don’t others hesitate before saying something kind?<br>Something vulnerable?<br>Something that cracks the surface we all wear?</p><p>It’s so easy to keep these things hidden.<br>To lock them away in the name of pride or fear or timing.</p><p>But I don’t want to hide anymore.<br>Even if my voice trembles.<br>Even if my timing’s off.<br>Even if they don’t say anything back.</p><p>Because saying it — <br>speaking from that raw, awkward, trembling place — <br>matters to me.</p><p>Even if no one else sees it.<br>Even if it lands weird.<br>Even if I blush with embarrassment after.</p><p>Maybe that’s okay.</p><p>Maybe that’s human.</p><p>And maybe…<br>this time, I’ll say it anyway.</p>
