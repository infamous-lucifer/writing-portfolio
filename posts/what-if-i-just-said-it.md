id="de38" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">I’m scared.<p id="d457" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">I want to send a message to the people in my life — <br>my colleagues, my friends, my parents.<br>I want to tell them what I see in them.<br>How they’ve impacted me.<br>How their presence, in small and quiet ways,<br>has shaped the way I see the world and myself.<p id="440f" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">But I’m scared.<p id="fe03" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">I’m scared they’ll think it’s fake.<br>That it’ll sound awkward.<br>That I’ll seem over-emotional.<br>That some won’t feel the same way,<br>or worse — won’t believe that it’s coming from my heart.<p id="961f" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">I imagine them reading it and cringing.<br>I imagine them thinking, “What is this?”<br>I imagine myself shrinking after I press send.<p id="b0d8" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">And yet…<p id="5658" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">It feels like something I have to say.<p id="2ec3" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Not to get something from them.<br>Not to be praised or validated.<br>But because it’s true.<br>Because I’ve carried these thoughts inside me for too long,<br>and maybe they deserve to live outside me for once.<p id="8275" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">You know that feeling when a kid writes something for class — <br>a poem, a letter, a little paragraph — <br>and they’re nervous about showing it to their parents?<br>Not because it’s wrong,<br>but because it’s real.<p id="ebaa" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">That’s how I feel.<p id="0bca" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Like I’ve scribbled something honest in a notebook,<br>and now I’m walking around with it in my hand,<br>unsure if I should pass it to the people it’s meant for.<p id="7476" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">And I wonder…<br>don’t others feel this way, too?<p id="c91f" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Don’t others hesitate before saying something kind?<br>Something vulnerable?<br>Something that cracks the surface we all wear?<p id="be23" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">It’s so easy to keep these things hidden.<br>To lock them away in the name of pride or fear or timing.<p id="5bf7" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">But I don’t want to hide anymore.<br>Even if my voice trembles.<br>Even if my timing’s off.<br>Even if they don’t say anything back.<p id="e4c7" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Because saying it — <br>speaking from that raw, awkward, trembling place — <br>matters to me.<p id="2ead" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Even if no one else sees it.<br>Even if it lands weird.<br>Even if I blush with embarrassment after.<p id="d8e6" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Maybe that’s okay.<p id="0779" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Maybe that’s human.<p id="3131" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p graf--trailing">And maybe…<br>this time, I’ll say it anyway.
