> _It doesn’t happen in some dramatic, life-altering decision.  
> It happens in the smallest of choices._

Like which lane to walk home through.

There I was, outside the hospital, fully grown, supposedly conscious — and yet, I was negotiating with myself about a route.

I didn’t want the faster one. I wanted the other. Just because.  
But logic came in. Guilt followed.

And suddenly, it wasn’t a simple walk home.  
It was a courtroom.  
And I had to **defend** my own desire…

Just to take the road I already wanted.

That’s how it happens.  
Not with fireworks — but with silent fights.

You think you’re free, but you have to _earn your way_ into your own small joys.  
And by the time you win, the joy feels… undeserved. Or too late.

The resistance doesn’t wait for some grand decision.  
It meets me the moment I wake up.

I open my eyes and I already hear it —

> _“You should go to the gym.”_

Not said with warmth. Not encouragement.  
Not, _“Let’s move your body, let’s care for ourselves today.”_

No — it comes with accusation.  
With guilt.  
With a slow-burning hatred dressed up as discipline.

It doesn’t sound like a knife.  
It sounds like logic.  
But it cuts all the same.

And that’s the most dangerous part.

These voices don’t scream. They _reason_.  
They tell me I’m weak for choosing softness.  
They call my joy irresponsible.

And I believe them — because they speak my language.

They don’t lead me with love.  
They lead me with fear.

Fear that if I choose peace, I’ll fall behind.  
Fear that if I rest, I’ll rot.  
Fear that if I _actually allow_ myself freedom…

…I might feel whole.  
And I won’t know what to do with that.

You think it’s only about small things.

A route.  
A skipped coffee.  
A hesitation before saying what I really feel.

But those voices? They’re practicing.

And before you even notice, they’ve moved into your **dreams**, your **career**, your **relationships**.

You start choosing jobs for safety.  
You stop liking people unless it’s “within your means.”  
You talk yourself out of trying, before anyone else gets the chance.

You don’t let yourself want — not because you’re above it,  
But because you’ve been _taught_ to feel shame for even hoping.

> _“How dare you?”_

That’s the tone.

How dare you think someone could love you.  
How dare you desire more than stability.  
How dare you believe in joy.

> Look at yourself. _Pathetic. Weak.  
>  No grit. No luck. No power._

And yet —

**Desire is human.**

Joy is not irrational.

Wanting something _even if it’s not logical_ is not foolish — it’s **evidence that you’re alive**.

So fight it.

Fight it like I do.  
In small choices.  
In stolen seconds of softness.  
In choosing _your lane home_ even when it doesn’t make sense.

Even when logic screams at you to obey —   
Choose something unreasonable, gentle, human.

_Choose something for you._

And maybe, one day, wanting will be enough.
