</figure><p>Maybe I’m not the right person for her.<br>If she can’t tell me what she feels — <br>if her love hides behind glances and silences,<br>if I have to decode her instead of simply be told — <br>then maybe I’m not the one she feels safe enough to be real with.</p><p>And that’s okay.</p><p>Because she deserves someone<br>who makes her forget she ever had to hide.<br>Someone she can say <em>“I like you”</em> or <em>“I love you”</em> to<br>without rehearsing it first in her head.<br>Without fear.</p><p>I don’t want a love that tests me.<br>I want a love that meets me.<br>Fully. Freely. Without disguise.</p><p>And I want to be someone<br>who makes another feel safe enough<br>to bring out their best self — <br>their softest thoughts, their tangled truths, their trembling honesty.</p><p>If I can’t be that space for her,<br>then maybe we aren’t the right fit.</p><p>She deserves someone she can fall apart with<br>and feel held, not judged.<br>And I deserve a love that doesn’t make me guess — <br>just opens its hands… and stays.</p>
