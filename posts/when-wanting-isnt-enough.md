</figure><blockquote><em>It doesn’t happen in some dramatic, life-altering decision.<br>It happens in the smallest of choices.</em></blockquote><p>Like which lane to walk home through.</p><p>There I was, outside the hospital, fully grown, supposedly conscious — and yet, I was negotiating with myself about a route.</p><p>I didn’t want the faster one. I wanted the other. Just because.<br>But logic came in. Guilt followed.</p><p>And suddenly, it wasn’t a simple walk home.<br>It was a courtroom.<br>And I had to <strong>defend</strong> my own desire…</p><p>Just to take the road I already wanted.</p><p>That’s how it happens.<br>Not with fireworks — but with silent fights.</p><p>You think you’re free, but you have to <em>earn your way</em> into your own small joys.<br>And by the time you win, the joy feels… undeserved. Or too late.</p>
