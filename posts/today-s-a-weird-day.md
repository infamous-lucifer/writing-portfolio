Today’s a weird day.  
Not because I woke up sad —   
but because I didn’t.

No ache in my chest.  
No urgency to be loved.  
No inner monologue begging for connection or attention or someone to miss me.

Just… quiet.

And it feels strange.  
Unsettling, even.

Not because I’m hurting —   
but because I’m not.

Because for so long, pain was the map.  
The familiar place I returned to.  
The ache was my identity.  
The longing gave me direction.

Now without it —   
Who am I?

Is this peace?  
Or is this what numbness disguised as healing looks like?

I don’t know.  
But I know this much:

There’s something terrifying about not knowing what to do with yourself  
when you’re no longer trying to survive.

Because I used to understand pain.  
I knew its rhythm.  
I knew how to write about it, cry through it, sit with it until it softened.

But this?  
This stillness?  
This emptiness?

It’s like walking through a room with no furniture, no memories, no sound —   
just blank space.

And a part of me —   
a stubborn, aching part —   
wants to run back into sadness.  
Because at least there,  
I knew what I was going through.

Today is weird.  
Because I realized I had made pain my home.

And now that it’s gone…  
I don’t know where I live anymore.
