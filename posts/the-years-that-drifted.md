You know,  
I’ve been sad.  
And lonely.  
And chaotic in ways I couldn’t even name back then.

It feels like I drifted for four years straight.  
Not growing.  
Not anchored.  
Just existing in a fog that no one else could see.

And then last year…  
everything changed — quietly.

My grandfather died.  
And something inside me collapsed.  
Not just grief for him —   
but a kind of exhaustion.  
Like I couldn’t pretend to care about the world anymore.  
Or even my family.  
Not in the same way.

But that was also the year  
something began to shift.

I still struggle.  
I still feel sad.  
I still feel insecure.

But I’m not in that dark place anymore.

That matters.  
That’s something.

I’ve started feeling like life is moving again.  
And somehow —   
so am I.

It’s not perfect.  
It’s not linear.  
But it’s no longer frozen.

There’s a future now,  
even if it’s chaotic,  
even if I fail a few times.

It’s _there_.  
And I can see it.

But if I’m honest…

I still feel lonely.

Not in the dramatic way.  
Just in that soft ache  
where you realize you haven’t laughed with someone in weeks —   
the way you used to in school.

Those friends who were always around?  
Now we talk once in months.  
Sometimes not even that.  
And I wonder:  
Would we even call each other again?

I miss what it felt like  
to belong without effort.

But I’ve started letting that go, too.  
Not bitterly.  
Just…accepting.

They’re busy.  
I’m busy.  
And life is happening —   
every single day.

I don’t know what the future holds.  
I really don’t.  
But I think something has finally ended in me.

Not just school.  
Not just college.

But the life that stretched from then until now —   
those four drifting years.  
That in-between era.

It’s closed.

And what’s coming next?  
I have no clue.  
But for the first time in a long time,

**I’m ready to meet it.**
