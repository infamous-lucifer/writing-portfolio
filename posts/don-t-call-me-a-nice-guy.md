Let’s stop calling us “nice guys.”

We didn’t show up in the comment section looking to be dissected —   
We came because something hurt.  
Because a sentence, a story, a scene  
hit too close to the wounds we carry quietly.

Maybe we’re not even “nice.”  
Maybe we’re just hurting.

Some of us were never taught self-respect.  
Some of us confuse passivity for kindness  
because standing up for ourselves always felt like conflict.  
Some of us are still clawing our way out of childhoods  
where being “good” was the only way to be safe.

So don’t call us nice guys.

Call us what we really are:

Men trying to survive without a manual.  
Men trying to love without knowing how.  
Men who apologize before speaking  
because we learned early that our wants didn’t matter.

We weren’t hurt by you.  
We were already hurting before we got here.  
You just touched what hadn’t healed yet.

So next time…  
Don’t start with “nice guy.”  
Start with what it really is.

Not a manipulation tactic.  
Not a character flaw.  
Not entitlement.

But maybe… a scar.
