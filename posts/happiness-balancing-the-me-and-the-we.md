#### The One thing We all want

Happiness is something we all want, isn’t it? It’s like the one thing that makes all this living worthwhile. But should happiness always come first in our decisions? I guess it depends on where you are in life.

#### When Responsibilities Are Absent

If you don’t have responsibilities, then yeah, happiness should absolutely be your top priority. I mean, you’re living this one life for yourself, right? Why not enjoy it?

#### When Responsibilities Take Center Stage

But when you have responsibilities — like family or kids — it’s not so simple anymore. You’re not just living for yourself; you’re also thinking about their future, their education, their happiness, their everything. That’s a lot, and you can’t just chase your own joy without considering them.

#### Creating Pockets of Joy

That said, it’s not like you should stop being happy. I think it’s about balance. You can still create little pockets of happiness here and there. Maybe it’s spending quality time with your family, pursuing your hobbies, or just laughing over something silly. Happiness doesn’t always have to be this grand, all-consuming thing. Sometimes, it’s in those small everyday moments that you make space for, even while handling your responsibilities. That balance? That’s where the magic is.

#### The Blend of External and Internal Happiness

For me, happiness is never just one thing. It’s a mix of what’s happening outside and how I’m feeling inside. Like, take external stuff — maybe you win something, someone compliments you, or you eat your favorite meal. Those things light you up, right? But then there’s the internal side — like when you’re already feeling good, and suddenly the world seems brighter. It’s all connected.

#### A Childhood Memory That Stuck

I remember this one time as a kid when I participated in a small knowledge competition. To be honest, I wasn’t even interested or prepared for it. But somehow, I ended up winning first place. That moment was pure happiness. Not because of the prize, but because I realized, “Wow, I actually know something. I’m capable.” That tiny external moment gave me this massive internal boost. It’s funny how that works, isn’t it?

![](/assets/images/posts/1*UgS0enqShi5ZKLIe7-mpxA.jpeg.jpg)

#### Me Time: My True Joy

If there’s one thing that always makes me happy, though, it’s my “me time.” For me, it’s all about those little self-treats. Like on salary day, I always get myself something special. Maybe it’s food I’ve been craving or a dessert I’ve been dreaming about. Sure, it might not be the smartest financial decision, but honestly, who cares? These little things keep me going. They’re my way of saying, “Hey, you worked hard. Here’s your reward.”

#### The Refresh Button

The same goes for salon visits. It’s not just about getting a haircut or a beard trim — it’s about feeling refreshed, like hitting a reset button. These aren’t just indulgences for me; they’re what keep me motivated and grounded.

#### Happiness With Others

That doesn’t mean I don’t love spending time with family and friends — I do. I love talking to my family about everything, explaining cool things to them, or just sharing a laugh with my friends. But, to be honest, I’m not always happy in those moments. Sometimes there are expectations, misunderstandings, or just the usual chaos of being around people. My “me time”? That’s pure. It’s when I’m happiest without any strings attached.

#### Still Searching for a Bigger Joy

One area I’m still figuring out is hobbies and work. I know some people find happiness in their passions or careers. They find something they love and turn it into what they do. I haven’t reached that clarity yet. I’m still exploring what could bring me that kind of joy.

![](/assets/images/posts/1*KA2ivBY2GFBIWYMAfDgnQw.jpeg.jpg)

#### Finding Joy in the Small Things

For now, I find happiness in the small stuff — like sharing moments with friends, talking to people, or just treating myself. But sometimes I wonder if there’s something bigger out there for me, something that could make me happy on a deeper level.

#### The Many Faces of Happiness

At the end of the day, I think happiness isn’t about one big thing. It’s a mix of all the little things — those self-treats, the shared laughs, the quiet moments. For some people, it’s in the life they build for their families. For others, it’s in the small joys they give themselves. For me, it’s a bit of both. And I think that’s okay. Happiness doesn’t have to look the same for everyone. It’s personal, and it’s worth chasing in whatever way feels right to you. After all, isn’t that what makes life beautiful?

*   Yours truly, Akshat Singh
