</figure><p>Let’s stop calling us “nice guys.”</p><p>We didn’t show up in the comment section looking to be dissected — <br>We came because something hurt.<br>Because a sentence, a story, a scene<br>hit too close to the wounds we carry quietly.</p><p>Maybe we’re not even “nice.”<br>Maybe we’re just hurting.</p><p>Some of us were never taught self-respect.<br>Some of us confuse passivity for kindness<br>because standing up for ourselves always felt like conflict.<br>Some of us are still clawing our way out of childhoods<br>where being “good” was the only way to be safe.</p><p>So don’t call us nice guys.</p><p>Call us what we really are:</p><p>Men trying to survive without a manual.<br>Men trying to love without knowing how.<br>Men who apologize before speaking<br>because we learned early that our wants didn’t matter.</p><p>We weren’t hurt by you.<br>We were already hurting before we got here.<br>You just touched what hadn’t healed yet.</p><p>So next time…<br>Don’t start with “nice guy.”<br>Start with what it really is.</p><p>Not a manipulation tactic.<br>Not a character flaw.<br>Not entitlement.</p><p>But maybe… a scar.</p>
