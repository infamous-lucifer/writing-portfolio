I think I need to confess my behavior.

Overly casual. Overly eager.  
Always trying to join.  
Trying to be their friend.  
Making jokes — the kind that land badly or don’t land at all.

I try to be the clown.  
Maybe I am the clown.  
So they like me.  
So they include me.  
So they choose me.

But underneath that performance, I’m scared.  
Because if I don’t do all this —   
If I don’t over-try —   
I fear they won’t see me at all.

I fear I won’t have anything to offer.  
Not resonance. Not connection.  
Not the depth people admire.  
Not the cleverness that earns respect.  
Not the strength or confidence that makes people listen.

So I try too hard, because if I stop trying —   
what’s left?

A quiet boy with no opinion, no charisma,  
no edge,  
no voice that naturally fits into group conversations.

I sit beside them and wonder:  
what do they see?

That guy who jokes too much?  
Who laughs too loudly at the wrong things?  
Who seems desperate to be liked —   
but isn’t?

And worst of all…  
I see it too.  
And I don’t know how to stop.

Because this is the only version of me  
that knows how to reach out.

Even if he’s reaching with a trembling hand,  
in a costume stitched together  
from all the things I wish I could be.
